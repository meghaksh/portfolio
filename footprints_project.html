MSU FootPrints: Web Development, Graphic Design

The Organization

FootPrints is a non-profit national level organization governed by the student body of The Maharaja Sayajirao University of Baroda. It is a three-day technical fest organized every year in February. Apart from the technical events of various engineering departments, FootPrints organizes non-technical events, guest lectures, workshops, night concerts, hackathons, as well as social work activities. From its inception in 2001, FootPrints has invited well-known personalities as keynote speakers from organizations including NASA, Microsoft, Tesla, Amazon, Aston Martin, HyperLoop One, etc.

Achievements

FootPrints holds a Guinness Book of World Record and Limca Book of Record for the largest paper bag created by team members of FootPrints. It has received patronages from UNESCO, SAYEN (South Asia Youth Environment Network) & CEE (Centre for Environment Education), Make in India & Skill India Campaign initiated by Hon'ble Prime Minister of India as well as appreciation letter from the Chief Minister of the state Gujarat, India.

My role

I joined FootPrints as an organizing team member in 2014. My role was to create an event website, design digital content for business and promotion, organize hackathons and technical events related to the computer science department, visit universities for the propagation of the event, bring sponsorships from the local companies, invite keynote speakers, manage press and electronic media conferences before and during the event. Later, I was promoted as part of the Core Committee member in 2017 and Event Coordinator in 2018. My role included management and leadership tasks to ensure the smooth execution of the event with the footfall of around 35000 students from all over India. I led a team of 150 members from various engineering backgrounds to run more than 56 departments of FootPrints.
